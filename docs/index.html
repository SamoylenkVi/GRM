Over the past two years we have seen events fueling inflation: COVID-19, money printing, geopolitical events. We expect high inflation levels to continue over the coming months and years.

Figure 1 shows the long-term commodities cycle over decades. In 2019 commodity prices have stabilized after a 10 years price depreciation (see the orange line).

During the pandemic period, in 2020-2022, and in present times, we can observe the continuation of disruptions of supply chains and imbalances in supply and demand.

We believe these circumstances are the start point for the reversal of the commodity price trend. We expect an appreciation of commodity prices for the periods ahead of us (see dotted blue line in graph).
